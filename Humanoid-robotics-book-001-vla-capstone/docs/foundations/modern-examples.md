# Modern Examples of Humanoid Robots

The field of humanoid robotics is rapidly advancing, with numerous research labs and companies developing increasingly sophisticated machines. Here are some prominent modern examples that illustrate the state of the art:

## 1. Boston Dynamics Atlas

![Boston Dynamics Atlas](/img/module-0/atlas.jpg) <!-- Placeholder image path -->

Atlas is one of the most agile humanoid robots ever built, renowned for its dynamic balance and athletic capabilities. Developed by Boston Dynamics, Atlas can run, jump, perform parkour, and recover from falls with remarkable fluidity. It serves as a research platform for advanced locomotion and manipulation.

-   **Key Features**: Hydraulic actuation, advanced balance control, whole-body manipulation.
-   **Capabilities**: Complex acrobatic maneuvers, handling heavy objects, navigating uneven terrain.

## 2. Tesla Optimus

![Tesla Optimus](/img/module-0/optimus.jpg) <!-- Placeholder image path -->

Tesla Optimus, also known as "Tesla Bot," is an ambitious project by Tesla AI aiming to develop a general-purpose humanoid robot for mundane, repetitive, and dangerous tasks. The goal is to create a robot that is mass-producible and affordable for wide adoption.

-   **Key Features**: Electric actuation, AI-driven control, designed for manufacturing scalability.
-   **Capabilities**: Conceptualized for factory work, domestic tasks, and general assistance.

## 3. Agility Robotics Digit

![Agility Robotics Digit](/img/module-0/digit.jpg) <!-- Placeholder image path -->

Digit, from Agility Robotics, is a bipedal robot designed for logistics applications, particularly in warehouses and last-mile delivery. It features arms for manipulation and a torso for carrying payloads, capable of working alongside humans.

-   **Key Features**: Legged mobility, torso with manipulator arms, designed for human environments.
-   **Capabilities**: Package delivery, warehouse tasks, walking on various terrains.

## 4. Unitree H1

![Unitree H1](/img/module-0/unitree_h1.jpg) <!-- Placeholder image path -->

Unitree Robotics, known for its quadruped robots, has also entered the humanoid space with the H1. This robot emphasizes high-performance joints and robust locomotion, aiming for versatility in different applications.

-   **Key Features**: High-torque electric motors, advanced joint design, dynamic walking.
-   **Capabilities**: Demonstrates strong walking and balance capabilities, potential for diverse tasks.

## 5. Sanctuary AI Phoenix

![Sanctuary AI Phoenix](/img/module-0/phoenix.jpg) <!-- Placeholder image path -->

Phoenix by Sanctuary AI is being developed as a general-purpose humanoid robot capable of performing a wide range of tasks. The company focuses on developing a cognitive architecture that allows the robot to learn and adapt to new tasks quickly.

-   **Key Features**: General-purpose design, emphasis on AI and cognitive capabilities.
-   **Capabilities**: Aimed at performing a variety of labor-intensive tasks.

These examples highlight the diverse approaches and rapid progress in humanoid robotics, from agility and strength to intelligence and widespread applicability.
